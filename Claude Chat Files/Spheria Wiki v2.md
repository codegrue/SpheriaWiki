# Spheria — Wiki
*by Cody Leet*

## Overall Plot

Spheria by Cody Leet tells parallel stories of political upheaval and rebellion on two worlds. On Earth, Max has created Spheria - a simulated world populated by crystalline beings called Polyans who evolve within a mysterious environment divided by a massive chasm called the Rift. The project is funded by billionaire Graham and managed by Dana Carter. On Spheria, the Polyan society consists of three castes: Leaders, Workers, and Soldiers, centered around a Source of light and energy.

The story reaches its climax when ambitious Soldier Fa·ro stages a coup, murdering the Council of Leaders to become absolute dictator. He enslaves the Workers to build an enormous tower reaching toward the Source. Meanwhile, on Earth, researcher Min discovers that Dana has been running an unauthorized military weapons program in a secret server room, selling Spheria technology to DARPA for personal profit and Costa Rica retirement land. Min realizes Dana murdered colleague Olivia to cover up this conspiracy.

On Spheria, Worker leader Sa·ma secretly organizes a rebellion. He builds a beam cannon weapon and engineers the tower's collapse, transforming it into a bridge across the previously impassable Rift - offering Workers escape from Fa·ro's tyranny. When Fa·ro's Soldiers pursue them, a climactic battle erupts. Worker Le·ma heroically allows herself to be fired from Sa·ma's cannon, stealing Fa·ro's artifact that controls the Zalisk beasts. She summons a Zalisk herd that decimates the Soldiers, allowing Workers to escape across the bridge to freedom.

On Earth, Dana traps both Min and Max in a hospital incinerator to silence them, but they escape by climbing through the exhaust system. A volunteer discovers them banging SOS in Morse code. During a project presentation to Graham, video evidence of Dana's confession plays, exposing her crimes. She pulls a gun when security arrives to arrest her. The parallel narratives explore themes of tyranny versus freedom, sacrifice for collective good, and the mirror between human and artificial intelligence evolution, culminating in both worlds facing reckoning with authoritarian power and the cost of liberation.

## Themes

- Tyranny and authoritarianism vs. freedom and democracy
- Sacrifice for the collective good
- Ingenuity and resourcefulness in the face of oppression
- Corporate/military corruption and betrayal
- The parallel evolution of human and artificial intelligence
- Revolution and organized resistance against dictatorship
- Murder and conspiracy to maintain power
- The price of liberation and crossing boundaries
- Visionary leadership (both noble like Sa·ma and corrupt like Fa·ro/Dana)
- Trust and deception among colleagues
- The ethics of using AI for military purposes
- Class struggle and caste systems

## Settings

### Earth

**Brussels, Belgium (Hôtel de Ville):** Tower of city hall in Brussels where Max speaks at the 25th Solvay Conference on Physics. Has balcony overlooking the city where Max smokes electronic cigarette with David.

**UConn Health Center:** Medical-dental school and research facility built in 1970 on highest point in Hartford County. Houses the Spheria Project. Features Lyman Maynard Stowe Library with 'Spheres' sculptures by Wopo Holop. Site of Tim's suicide.

**Spheria Project Office:** Research facility within UConn Health Center where team observes and studies the Spheria simulation. Features makeshift lobby/reception area, cubicles, conference room, and spaces for team happy hours.

**UConn Health Center rooftop:** Roof of the Health Center building with mooring mast disguised as cell tower; docking point for Graham's airship; accessed via emergency stairwell

**Airship 'Buruwa Gunya':** Graham's enormous yellow luxury airship with suspended gondola; open floor plan with sunken booths, kitchen, private rear rooms, and slanted wrap-around windows

**Electric Boat Pier 3, Groton, CT:** Dark pier at Shaw Cove with Virginia-class submarine, yellow crane, and cargo containers; meeting location in rain

**Secret red server room:** Hidden duplicate server room beneath the main server floor with red lighting, connected via submarine-style hatch, running unauthorized military computing operations

**Hospital incinerator chamber:** Domed chamber at base of hospital incinerator where Min and Max become trapped with a corpse on a gurney

### Spheria

**Spheria (The World/Sphere):** A computer-simulated world shaped like a sphere with a bright light source called 'The Source' at its center. Divided into maze-like sections by impassable blue crystal mountains that emit repulsive force. Features red rivers, brown rock ground, green vegetation, and various colored crystals. Has two hemispheres separated by the Rift.

**The Colony:** The central settlement of the Polyans where they build hives and organize their society by castes (Soldiers, Drones, Builders, Leaders, Lumen Seekers).

**Uplift Pass:** A large slit/crevice in the blue crystal mountain that allows passage through. Named by ancestors, used by Lumen Masters to take apprentices for training. Features platforms that can be climbed.

**The Rift:** A massive vertical formation that divides the world into two hemispheres. Acts as an artificial horizon. Yellow shards thrown into it turn into bubbles. Site of Le·ma's final test.

**Valley of Three:** A large perfect triangle formation of mountains on the opposite hemisphere with no rivers inside. Used for navigation at night. Sometimes shows flickering dots believed to be gods playing games.

**Lumen Grotto:** A hexagonal room inside a hill formation far from the Colony. Contains a glowing yellow crystal. Where young Sa·ma met his mentor and began his path as a Lumen Seeker.

**The Fertile Field:** A legendary field of endless energy that Sa·ma discovers. Not always visible - appears and disappears. Where Sa·ma gains his mysterious indigo sensor artifact.

**The Gold Cave:** Large cave discovered by Sa·ma on hillside beyond the river; contains glowing plants and mysterious portal to 'Qubessence'; floor appears to be made of gold; inhabited by cave god

**Council Prison Cell:** Triangular room inside Council building that tapers to a point; originally shelter against Zalisk attacks; now used as first Polyan prison; contains hexagonal pit in center

**The bridge (fallen tower):** The tower converted into a bridge across the Rift after being toppled by Sa·ma's beam weapon

## Factions

### Polyan Castes (Spheria)
Social structure dividing Polyans by number of legs and role: Soldiers (5 legs), Drones (3 legs), Builders (4 legs), Leaders (6 legs), and Lumen Seekers (special knowledge keepers)
Key Members: Le·ma, Sa·ma, Soldiers, Drones, Builders, Leaders

### Spheria Project Team (Earth)
Scientific research team at UConn Health Center studying the Spheria computer simulation. Described as socially awkward scientists who bond over happy hours.
Key Members: Max, Min, Jean, Tim (deceased), David

### Lumen Seekers (Spheria)
Elite group of Polyans who possess secret knowledge about the world passed down through generations. Led by Lumen Master. Status equal to or higher than six-legged Leaders.
Key Members: Sa·ma (Lumen Master), Le·ma (apprentice), Sa·ma's mentor (elderly, in Lumen Grotto)

### Fa·ro's followers (Spheria)
Polyans who support Fa·ro's increasing authority and decision-making power in the Council; view him as bringing strength and decisiveness to previously gridlocked leadership
Key Members: Fa·ro, Ga·zo, Za·zo

### DARPA conspirators (Earth)
Military intelligence group that made secret deal with Dana to access Spheria technology despite Graham's contract restrictions
Key Members: Dana Carter, unnamed DARPA officials

### Worker Rebellion (Spheria)
All Workers in the Colony who secretly organized to escape Fa·ro's tyranny by building and toppling the tower to cross the Rift
Key Members: Sa·ma, Le·ma, Hi·ma, Pu·ma, all Colony Workers

## Key Objects

### Violet crystals
Life-giving crystals that serve as the core/heart of all Spherian creatures. Without them, creatures would be mere inanimate connected tetrahedrons.
Significance: Essential for all life in Spheria; primary hunting target for Polyans

### The Source
A bright white shining orb above the world that governs creature behavior and provides light. Changes color to affect Spherian activity. The central light at the heart of the sphere-world.
Significance: Acts as sun and behavioral controller for all Spherians

### Blue crystal mountains
Mountains made of blue crystal that emit a repulsive force, making them impassable and dividing the world into maze-like sections.
Significance: Create the geography and boundaries of the Spherian world

### Green crystals
Crystals containing seed energy that creatures like Zalisks dig up and consume from the ground.
Significance: Food source for herbivorous Spherian creatures

### Yellow crystals/shards
Crystals used by Polyans for light and energy. When thrown into the Rift, they transform into yellow bubbles. Found in the Lumen Grotto.
Significance: Energy source for Polyans; used in rituals and teaching

### Red rivers
Glowing red rivers that flow through the Spherian landscape, visible from the opposite hemisphere at night.
Significance: Major geographical features used for navigation

### Indigo sensor
A mysterious artifact sensor with three triangles pointing in different directions. Gained by Sa·ma from the Fertile Field. Allows control over other creatures.
Significance: Grants extraordinary powers; will be passed from master to apprentice

### Spy glasses
Thick-rimmed glasses purchased from spygear.com that Min wears constantly. They record everything she sees.
Significance: Allows Min to document her experiences; filmed her interview

### VR headset and glove
Virtual reality equipment used by Jean and team to observe Spheria. Works with 3D SpaceMouse controller for navigation.
Significance: Interface allowing humans to observe but not interact with Spheria

### 'Spheres' sculptures
Bronze sculptures by artist Wopo Holop at UConn Health Center, depicting cell division, human retina, Earth, acorn, DNA strand, and faceted/smooth spheres.
Significance: Site where Tim ties his shoe before his suicide; thematically connected to the Spheria project

### A Short History of Nearly Everything
Book by Bill Bryson that Max reads at least once a year. Blue cover with small Earth photo and asterisk.
Significance: Max's favorite book; represents his passion for understanding everything

### Mooring mast
Structure on UConn Health Center roof that appears to be a cell tower antenna but serves as airship docking point; part of Graham's grant stipulation
Significance: Allows Graham's airship to dock at the facility

### Seven-sided core crystal
Empty crystal prepared for creating a second seven-legged Polyan god
Significance: Part of the ritual to create another god from a violet bubble

### Seven orange elongated crystals
Leg crystals placed near each facet of the seven-sided core crystal
Significance: Used in the god-creation ceremony alongside the core crystal

### Yellow shard
Small glowing crystal used for light in the prison cell
Significance: Provides illumination in the dark triangular prison where Sa·ma is held

### Blue rock wall
Wall constructed along the red river designed by Sa·ma to protect Colony hives
Significance: Prevents the expanding river from destroying bordering Colony structures

### Glowing cave plants
Plants in the gold cave that emit light beams when broken; can heal themselves
Significance: Create light maze in cave; broken ones previously showed portal but healed

### Submarine hatch/manhole cover
Heavy circular hatch with locking wheel connecting the main server floor to the secret red room below
Significance: Access point to Dana's unauthorized military server operation

### Sa·ma's beam cannon
Blue crystal weapon that fires compressed Polyans or energy beams, used to destroy tower supports and later fire Le·ma at Fa·ro
Significance: Key weapon that enables Workers to topple the tower and turn the tide of battle

### Fa·ro's artifact
Device allowing Fa·ro to see living creatures as violet outlines and control Zalisk herds
Significance: Symbol of Fa·ro's power; its theft by Le·ma allows Workers to turn Zalisk against Soldiers

### Video confession recording
Recording of Dana confessing to Olivia's murder and DARPA conspiracy
Significance: Evidence that exposes Dana's crimes and military weapons deal

## Chapter Synopses

### Ch. 1: Shard of Life [Spherian]
Nine Polyan Soldiers hunt a massive Zalisk beast. The Zalisk, like all Spherian creatures, is animated by a violet crystal core. The Polyans work as a team, having learned from past deaths, to surround and potentially take down the creature for its life-giving crystal.

### Ch. 2: 1% Inspiration, 99% Funding [Human]
Max steps onto a balcony in Brussels after successfully presenting at the 25th Solvay Conference on Physics. His friend David, the conference chair, congratulates him. Max reflects on feeling like an observer of society and his passion for understanding how humans are all connected.

### Ch. 3: Uplift Pass [Spherian]
Sa·ma leads his apprentice Le·ma on a journey far from the Colony to Uplift Pass, a crevice through the blue crystal mountains. He tells her she will gain knowledge known only to select few and will someday replace him as Lumen Master, achieving status equal to or higher than the six-legged Leaders.

### Ch. 4: Cell Division [Human]
Timothy and Jill approach UConn Health Center library to study. Tim stops to tie his shoe by the 'Spheres' sculptures depicting cell division and other biological forms. Jill suddenly screams as Tim is splattered with blood - he has witnessed or been hit by something traumatic involving these sculptures.

### Ch. 5: Arcane Physics [Spherian]
Le·ma watches Sa·ma jump off the mountain, navigating down the steep slope by bouncing off outcroppings. She reluctantly follows, tumbling over the edge but learning to glide using the repulsive force of the blue crystal. She lands safely and they head toward the Rift.

### Ch. 6: First Impressions [Human]
Max waits impatiently for a slow elevator to reach an interview, frustrated that kids pressed all the buttons. He mentally complains about the lack of ability to deselect elevator buttons, comparing it to computer interfaces. He's running late at 10:05 for an important intern interview - the team has been without one for six weeks after their previous intern's tragic suicide.

### Ch. 7: The Interview [Human]
Min comes home and tells her roommate Sheila about her internship interview for the Spheria Project. She explains she'll be watching a virtual ecosystem with computer creatures called Spherians, specifically intelligent ones called Polyans. Min filmed the interview with her spy glasses and offers to show Sheila the footage.

### Ch. 8: The Rift [Spherian]
Sa·ma and Le·ma make camp beside the massive Rift formation. As darkness falls, they observe the opposite hemisphere with its blue mountain ranges and red rivers. Le·ma asks about the Valley of Three triangle formation. Sa·ma explains nobody knows its purpose but gods are said to play games there. He promises to show her an even larger formation.

### Ch. 9: First Day [Human]
Min starts her first day at the Spheria Project. Max shows her to the reception desk which will be her workstation - she'll rarely have receptionist duties since visitors are infrequent. Max promises interesting projects despite the socially awkward science team. At 11am team meeting, Min meets the full staff.

### Ch. 10: The Fertile Field [Spherian]
Flashback to young Sa·ma exploring far from the Colony. He discovers the Lumen Grotto, a hexagonal room with a glowing yellow crystal and an old unknown Polyan. The elder says he expected Sa·ma, revealing this is the first test - only the excessively confident venture this far. The elder begins teaching Sa·ma the path to becoming a Lumen Seeker.

### Ch. 11: Min's Assignment [Human]
On an early workday, Min gets an instant message from Max to see him. In his office, she browses his bookshelf while he prepares files. She finds his favorite book, 'A Short History of Nearly Everything' by Bill Bryson. Max explains he reads it yearly and wishes for an updated edition covering quantum physics, noting they are creating history with their work.

### Ch. 12: Return Journey [Spherian]
Le·ma wakes to find Sa·ma has left her alone at the Rift during the night. She sees a yellow bubble rising from the depths, possibly the shard they threw in. She begins the return journey, moving slower to observe her surroundings. At a peaceful spot, she reflects on her training and readiness to someday replace Sa·ma.

### Ch. 13: Happy Hour [Human]
At 6:12pm, Min finishes work and joins the team's happy hour gathering. She contributes Vietnamese Goi Cuon (salad rolls) with Nuoc cham sauce. Max tries and loves them. Tim makes frozen drinks in a blender. The team bonds over ethnic food and drinks in the office space between cubicles.

### Ch. 14: Extra Sense [Spherian]
A Zalisk is about to kill Le·ma when Sa·ma's voice commands it to turn around and leave, which it obeys. Sa·ma reveals he has gained a new indigo sensor - an artifact gift from the gods that gives greater insight. He discovered the legendary Fertile Field and gained this sensor there. It allows him to control other creatures and will be passed to Le·ma someday.

### Ch. 15: Starlight Walk [Human]
After drinking, Max and Min walk through the UConn Health Center parking lot at night. Max is very drunk, staggering and seeing parked cars as boats. He sits in an unlocked Mercedes. Min tries to get him out, worried about security. Max opens the door but the chapter ends mid-action.

### Ch. 16: Cliffhanger [Spherian]
Le·ma and Sa·ma walk back toward the blue mountains. Sa·ma reveals there's no opening or platforms on this side - the mountain is nearly vertical. The traditional Lumen Walk requires apprentices to figure out how to return, taking 3-4 days. Le·ma quickly realizes she must create a way over. They head to their bridge over the red river.

### Ch. 17: God from the Machine [Human]
Jean returns from lunch energized and dons VR headset and glove to observe Spheria. She uses a 3D SpaceMouse controller to fly through the world, performing her ritual approach to the Polyan Colony via the Rift. She watches Drones and Builders constructing a new igloo-like hive. She wants to help but is forbidden to interact - only engineers can, and only away from Polyans.

### Ch. 18: Prime Directive [Human]
Dana reviews weekly project logs showing increased unusual Polyan activity. Anthropology log entries exceed engineering for first time, indicating Polyans are moving toward a 'new social zeitgeist' and doing incomprehensible things.

### Ch. 19: Crossing Over [Human]
Max drives to UConn Health Center, late again. He's shocked to see Graham's enormous yellow airship 'Buruwa Gunya' moored atop the building. He observes the Ponzo Illusion making the airship appear larger from a distance.

### Ch. 20: Running Out [Human]
Dana and Max climb emergency stairs to the roof and board Graham's luxury airship. The interior is massive with open floor plan, sunken booths, kitchen, and slanted windows offering impressive views of Farmington Valley.

### Ch. 21: Cat and Mouse [Human]
A mysterious driver in a black sedan arrives at Electric Boat Pier 3 in Groton during a rainstorm. They park near a Virginia-class submarine and cargo containers beneath a yellow crane in the darkness.

### Ch. 22: In Our Image [Spherian]
Sa·ma reports the fourth consecutive red bubble rising past the Rift. The eight Leaders (Wu·sa, Yo·sa, Me·sa, Ju·sa, Ki·sa, Co·sa, Pi·sa, Vu·sa) debate whether to move the Colony as the river threatens to consume hives. They question if the gods are angry.

### Ch. 23: Nourishment [Human]
Max and Min have dinner and discuss religion. Min was raised Catholic by emigrant parents but doesn't dwell on it. Max views the universe as mathematical/algorithmic - if God exists, he just created fundamental rules and is watching the results unfold.

### Ch. 24: Pro Creation [Human]
Min visits Max's minimalist apartment with impressive entertainment center and game systems. His bedroom has walls covered in bookcases. They decide to play Minecraft together and discuss the game's lack of story versus creating your own narrative.

### Ch. 25: Start the Press [Human]
Gizmodo article by Moses Diego about visiting the lab. He describes the hallway of Polyan posters, learns about their caste system based on leg count, and the seven-legged god. Article discusses the virtual world called 'Spheria' and the diverse research team.

### Ch. 26: Conspiracy [Spherian]
Fa·ro surveys the empty Colony center from the central dais, frustrated that he's not looked down upon by the Leaders despite being a god. He poses imposingly for a watching Drone and contemplates having Sa·ma build him a statue.

### Ch. 27: The World Builder [Human]
Min scrutinizes number lists when Frankie startles her. They debate whether the world is mathematical formulas (Min) or magical natural beauty (Frankie). Frankie invites her to go gather textures and see 'real beauty' in random patterns.

### Ch. 28: Streets of Gold [Spherian]
Sa·ma returns to Colony in a daze and gathers audience of Drones including Ti·ni and Po·ni. He tells them about discovering a cave with a hole in a hillside while searching for building materials. The cave was unlike any other he'd entered before.

### Ch. 29: The Deleted File [Human]
Olivia Holland's work journal entries reveal her first days as intern, breakup with Randy via email after 6 years, getting drunk with roommate Ashley, struggling with hangover at work, and growing closer to Ashley through the difficult period.

### Ch. 30: God Like You [Spherian]
Sa·ma reluctantly leads Fa·ro, Ga·zo, and Za·zo into the cave to find the portal. Fa·ro insists he must 'reunite with his brethren' in the Qubessence, believing it's his destiny to join the other gods rather than remain trapped on Spheria's surface.

### Ch. 31: The Exchange [Human]
Ashley is home alone during a thunderstorm watching The Walking Dead. She makes Dr. Pepper in a beaker and settles on the couch. During a tense scene with Rick Grimes, there's a knocking sound that Rick doesn't react to on screen.

Sa·ma is imprisoned in the triangular Council building cell (former Zalisk shelter, now first Polyan prison). Ga·zo visits with a yellow shard for light. They argue about Fa·ro's influence and the Colony's future - Ga·zo praises Fa·ro's decisiveness while Sa·ma warns nothing is unstoppable.

### Ch. 33: Coffee Break [Human]
Min works late alone preparing for Graham's visit tomorrow. Her eyes blur from fatigue. She makes coffee from the Keurig, but returns to find her computer has crashed with a blue 'System Failure' screen that won't respond to any commands.

### Ch. 34: Putsch [Spherian]
The eight Leaders and Fa·ro prepare to create another seven-legged god using a violet bubble. Ga·zo convinced them a backup god would be wise. Everything is set up with seven-sided core crystal and seven orange leg crystals. The violet Source triggers replication urge in all Polyans including Fa·ro.

Min discovers a secret red-lit duplicate server room beneath the main server floor, accessed through a submarine-style hatch. She finds cables descending through the floor and a red pipe from the Qube incinerator leading below.

Fa·ro consolidates power by eliminating the Council of Leaders. He declares himself sole ruler of the Colony and commands Ga·zo and Za·zo to present the captive Sa·ma. Fa·ro announces the Council's death to all Polyans, blaming their failed attempt to create another god.

Min accesses the computer in the secret red room using Olivia's password (6EQUJ5 from the Wow! Signal). She discovers Gridway meta-scheduling software and Tor network connections, suggesting unauthorized distributed computing and dark web activity.

### Ch. 38: The Plan [Spherian]
Hi·ma and Pu·ma follow Sa·ma's secret instructions to meet with twelve lead Workers in a hidden location. Flashback reveals Sa·ma showing Fa·ro the tower design and secretly planning resistance with Workers.

### Ch. 39: Mala Sanctis [Human]
Min is trapped in the red server room with Max, who has discovered her there. She kicks the cabinet door into his face and tries to escape. They fight, knocking loose hundreds of Qubes. Min barely escapes as Max pursues her.

### Ch. 40: The Machine [Spherian]
Ga·zo reports to Fa·ro that all Workers have vanished overnight. Using his artifact, Fa·ro sees them gathered beyond the tower as violet outlines. He orders the Soldiers to pursue and destroy the Workers.

### Ch. 41: Button Mashup [Human]
Min bursts into Dana's office claiming Max is building a super weapon and that Olivia was murdered for discovering it. Dana locks the door and they discuss the situation when Max arrives, forcing a confrontation.

### Ch. 42: Storm Wind [Spherian]
Ga·zo leads Soldiers in pursuit of the Workers. They encounter a mysterious wall of vertical blue rock stretching perpendicular to their path - a barrier the Workers have created to block them.

### Ch. 43: The Incinerator [Human]
Max, Min, and Dana's confrontation is interrupted when Dana reveals she was following them. Max denies involvement in the secret room. Dana traps both Max and Min in the hospital incinerator, attempting to murder them.

### Ch. 44: The Great Divide [Spherian]
Sa·ma activates his beam weapon, destroying two tower support columns. The tower falls across the Rift, creating a bridge. Workers begin crossing while Soldiers approach for battle.

### Ch. 45: Ashes to Ashes [Human]
Min and Max are trapped in the incinerator chamber with a corpse. They escape by using a gurney as a ladder to reach the exhaust vent. Min slides down the chimney and finds a clean-out door, banging on it in Morse code SOS.

### Ch. 46: Into Battle [Spherian]
Battle erupts as Soldiers massacre Workers. Le·ma volunteers to be fired from Sa·ma's cannon. She shoots through the air and lands on Fa·ro, stealing his artifact that controls the Zalisk.

### Ch. 47: Clean-out [Human]
Hospital volunteer Walter hears banging from the incinerator chimney while doing laundry. He follows the SOS pattern and opens the clean-out door, rescuing Min and Max.

### Ch. 48: Liberation [Spherian]
Le·ma uses Fa·ro's stolen artifact to summon a Zalisk herd that attacks only the Soldiers. The Workers escape across the bridge while the Zalisk decimate Fa·ro's army. Fa·ro is pinned by a Zalisk.

### Ch. 50: Interruptions [Human]

### Ch. 51: Hand of God [Mixed]
