Novel Wiki  ·  by Cody Leet  ·  51 Chapters

📖 Overview

Spheria concludes with parallel climaxes on Earth and the alien world. On Earth, Min discovers Dana's secret red server room running unauthorized DARPA weapons projects using Spheria technology. Dana had murdered Olivia for discovering the same truth and made a deal with the military for funding and personal gain, betraying Graham's pacifist vision. When confronted, Max (confused about the room's existence) traps Min and Dana in the hospital incinerator, but they escape with help from a hospital volunteer. At the board meeting, Max exposes Dana with a recorded confession video showing her admitting to Olivia's murder and the military deal. When campus security arrives, Dana draws a gun. On Spheria, Fa·ro has eliminated the Council and declared himself dictator, but Sa·ma has secretly organized a Worker rebellion. Sa·ma built a revolutionary beam cannon and used it to topple the Colony's massive tower across the Rift, creating the first bridge to the forbidden lands. As Fa·ro's Soldiers massacre fleeing Workers, Le·ma is launched from Sa·ma's cannon, crashes into Fa·ro, and steals his artifact. She uses it to control a Zalisk herd that decimates the Soldiers while ignoring Workers, enabling mass escape across the bridge. The Workers achieve liberation from tyranny by reaching lands previously thought unreachable, while Fa·ro lies pinned beneath a Zalisk, his reign collapsing. The Spheria team watches these dramatic events unfold on their screens as the simulation reveals unprecedented developments in both civilizations.

🗺 Settings

🌍

Earth

Hôtel de Ville de Bruxelles
Tower building in Brussels, Belgium where 25th Solvay Conference on Physics took place. Has balcony overlooking city.
UConn Health Center
Medical-dental school in Hartford County built 1970 on highest point. Expanded in 90s for clinical research. Houses Spheria Project. Contains Lyman Maynard Stowe Library with 'Spheres' sculptures.
Spheria Project Office
Research facility with makeshift lobby/reception desk, cubicles, conference room, kitchen. Team holds happy hour gatherings. Min works at reception desk with three large monitors.
Graham's Airship 'Buruwa Gunya'
A massive yellow luxury airship belonging to Graham, moored to the UConn Health Center roof via a disguised cell tower antenna. Features a suspended gondola with open floor plan, sunken booths, kitchen with stainless steel appliances, slanted wraparound windows, and private rooms toward the rear.
Electric Boat Pier 3, Groton CT
Submarine manufacturing facility at Shaw Cove with docks for Virginia-class submarines. Features a large yellow crane for lifting cargo and containers, minimally lit at night. Used for clandestine meetings.
Secret red server room
Hidden duplicate server room beneath the Qube floor with red lighting, containing unauthorized Tor network equipment and Gridway distributed computing system for DARPA weapons project
Hospital incinerator chamber
Domed propane-fueled incinerator where Min and Max become trapped; contains exhaust vent leading to chimney with clean-out door
🔮

Spheria

The Source
The bright shining orb above the Polyan world that governs behavior and provides light. Changes color (white for travel, fades at night). Contains the entire Spherian world.
Polyan Colony
Home settlement of the Polyans. Contains hives (igloo-like pointed structures). Center gathering place. Location where Drones, Builders, Soldiers, and Leaders live.
Blue Mountains
Mountain ranges made of blue crystal that emit repulsive force, making them impassable. Divide world into maze-like sections. Nearly vertical on one side.
Uplift Pass
Large slit/crevice in blue mountains containing platforms that allow passage to other side. Contains steps. Named by ancestors. Used for Lumen Seeker training.
The Rift
Massive chasm at edge of world. Appears as vertical artificial horizon. So deep the bottom cannot be seen. Yellow shards dropped in eventually become bubbles rising back up.
Valley of Three
Large perfect triangle formation of mountains on opposite hemisphere with no rivers inside. Used for navigation at night. Legends say gods play games there (flickering dots sometimes visible).
Red Rivers
Rivers of red liquid/energy that flow through Spherian landscape. Can be crossed with bridges made from brown rock. Illuminate blue mountains at night.
Lumen Grotto
Hexagonal room cut into hill-like formation far from Colony. Resting place where old Lumen Master waits to test potential Lumen Seekers. Contains glowing yellow crystal.
Fertile Field
Legendary location of endless energy. Not always visible/present. Sa·ma discovered it and received artifact sensor there. Considered a gift from the gods.
The Golden Cave
A mysterious cave discovered by Sa·ma containing golden crystalline formations, strange glowing plants, and a portal to the 'Qubessence.' Features twisting maze-like passages with light-producing plants that can be broken to create beams. Contains a room with a portal that Sa·ma encounters a cave god through.
Council Building Prison Cell
Triangular chamber tapering to a point, the hollow interior of the Council building. Originally a shelter against Zalisk attacks, later used for storage, now repurposed as the Polyans' first prison. Features a single large door, no windows, and a shallow hexagonal pit in the center.
The bridge (fallen tower)
The massive tower knocked down by Sa·ma's cannon to span across the Rift, creating the first bridge to the forbidden side
Fa·ro's Chamber
The apex chamber of the tallest structure in the Colony, formerly the Council chamber, now Fa·ro's throne room after he eliminated the Council

⚔️ Factions

Polyan Castes
Spherian
Social hierarchy with specialized roles: 3-legged Drones (workers), 4-legged Builders (construction), 5-legged Soldiers (hunters/warriors), 6-legged Leaders (governance). Females have swirling patterns, males have straight lines.
Members: Le·ma, Sa·ma, Soldier hunting party, Drones, Builders, Leaders
Lumen Seekers
Spherian
Elite group who possess special knowledge about the world. Only confident Polyans who venture far from Colony can find Lumen Grotto and be tested. Lumen Master has status equal to or higher than Leaders.
Members: Sa·ma (current Master), Le·ma (apprentice), Old Lumen Master (previous)
Spheria Project Team
Earth
Scientists at UConn Health Center studying virtual ecosystem with computer-generated creatures called Spherians/Polyans. Mix of physicists, programmers, and researchers. Hold happy hour gatherings.
Members: Max (lead scientist), Min (intern), Tim, Jean, David (external colleague)
Fa·ro's Faction
Spheria
Growing authoritarian movement centered on Fa·ro as a decisive god-leader who breaks Council gridlock. Represents shift from democratic consensus to autocratic rule, with Ga·zo as primary enforcer.
Members: Fa·ro, Ga·zo, Za·zo
Rebellious Workers
Spheria
All Workers in the Colony who secretly organized under Sa·ma's leadership to flee across the Rift using the fallen tower as a bridge, escaping Fa·ro's tyranny
Members: Sa·ma, Hi·ma, Pu·ma, Le·ma, twelve lead Workers
DARPA partnership
Earth
Secret military collaboration arranged by Dana with Defense Advanced Research Projects Agency to weaponize the Spheria technology in exchange for funding and personal land in Costa Rica
Members: Dana Carter, unnamed DARPA officials

💎 Key Objects

Violet Core Crystal
Life-giving crystal at center of all Spherian beings. Powers their existence - without it they are inanimate tetrahedron structures. Large ones found in Zalisks are valuable.
Significance: Fundamental to all Spherian life; object of Polyan hunts
Green Crystals
Buried crystals containing seed energy that Zalisks forage for by digging with tusks.
Significance: Food source for herbivorous Spherian creatures
Yellow Shards/Crystals
Small yellow crystals that provide light and energy. Can be dropped into Rift where they eventually transform into rising bubbles. Found in Lumen Grotto.
Significance: Energy source for Polyans; used in rituals and tests
Blue Mountain Crystals
Blue crystals that emit repulsive force field, making them impossible to touch. Form impassable mountain ranges that divide the world.
Significance: Natural barriers that shape geography and limit exploration
Artifact Sensor
Special indigo sensor with three triangles pointing in different directions. Attached to Sa·ma's core. Gives extraordinary abilities including commanding Zalisks.
Significance: Divine gift from Fertile Field; passed from master to apprentice; enhances Lumenary abilities
Brown Rock Blocks
Loose inert brown rock material pushed by Drones and stacked by Builders to construct hives and bridges.
Significance: Primary construction material in Spherian world
Spy Glasses
Thick-rimmed glasses from spygear.com that Min wears constantly. Record everything she sees and hears.
Significance: Allow Min to document her experiences; she films her interview
VR Headset and 3D SpaceMouse
Virtual reality equipment with glove that allows team to observe and navigate inside Spheria. SpaceMouse looks like truck towing ball with programmable buttons.
Significance: Primary interface for observing Spherian world; only engineers allowed to interact
'Spheres' Sculptures
Art installations by Wopo Holop at UConn library entrance. Include bronze objects: human retina, dividing frog eggs, Earth, acorn, DNA strand, faceted and smooth spheres.
Significance: Location of Jill's suicide; thematic connection to project
Seven-sided Core Crystal
Empty crystal structure used in the creation of seven-legged Polyans/gods, surrounded by seven orange elongated crystals near each facet
Significance: Central component in the ritual to create another god to accompany Fa·ro, representing the Polyans' attempt to control or replicate divine creation
Blue Rock Wall
Defensive wall constructed along the red river to protect Colony hives from destruction
Significance: One of Sa·ma's insightful designs demonstrating his engineering abilities and value to the Colony despite his imprisonment
Glowing Cave Plants
Strange plants in the golden cave that produce light and can be broken to create directional light beams; they heal themselves over time
Significance: Mark pathways through the cave maze and can be manipulated to reveal or conceal the portal to the Qubessence
Yellow Shard
Small glowing crystal used as portable light source
Significance: Provides minimal illumination in Sa·ma's prison cell, highlighting his reduced circumstances
Red server room cables and equipment
Bundle of red cables and unauthorized Tor network/Gridway software running in secret duplicate server room beneath the Qube floor
Significance: Evidence of Dana's secret DARPA weapons project and unauthorized military collaboration
Sa·ma's beam cannon
Device created by Sa·ma that fires energy beams capable of destroying tower support columns and launching Polyans through the air
Significance: Revolutionary technology that enables the Workers' rebellion by creating the bridge across the Rift and functioning as a weapon
Fa·ro's artifact
Device that allows Fa·ro to see through obstructions and control Zalisk; stolen by Le·ma during battle
Significance: Key to controlling the Zalisk herd and turning the tide of battle against the Soldiers
Incinerator gurney with corpse
Metal medical gurney with embalmed cadaver used as makeshift ladder inside the incinerator chamber
Significance: Provides escape route for Min and Max through exhaust vent to chimney
Blue rock barrier wall
Long continuous vertical wall of blue crystalline material creating a clearing perpendicular to the Soldiers' path
Significance: Defensive fortification created by Workers to slow Soldier pursuit and protect their escape route
