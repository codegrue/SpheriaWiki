## Overall Plot

Spheria is a parallel narrative following humans developing virtual reality technology to observe crystalline aliens (Polyans) on another world. On Earth, the Spheria Project team led by Max and Dana works under billionaire Graham's funding. Researcher Min discovers Dana has created a secret server room running dark web operations for DARPA weapons development. Dana murdered colleague Olivia who discovered this conspiracy and attempts to kill Min and Max by trapping them in a hospital incinerator. They escape with help from volunteer Walter. During a board meeting, Dana's confession video plays, exposing her deal with DARPA for Costa Rica land in exchange for weaponizing the project. Captain Brennan arrests Dana at gunpoint. On Spheria, tyrannical Leader Fa·ro eliminates the Council to become sole dictator and enslaves the Workers to build an enormous tower reaching toward the Source. Brilliant Worker Sa·ma secretly organizes rebellion, designing a beam cannon that topples the tower across the great Rift chasm, creating a bridge to freedom. During Fa·ro's massacre of Workers, Le·ma allows herself to be shot from Sa·ma's cannon, crashing into Fa·ro and stealing his artifact that controls the Zalisk beasts. The Zalisk then attack only Soldiers, allowing the Workers to escape across the bridge to unexplored territory. The humans observe these events, witnessing the first Polyan prayer-like behavior and recognizing the Workers' remarkable resourcefulness and desire for liberty, mirroring humanity's own struggles against tyranny and corruption.

## Themes

- Tyranny versus liberty and the fight for freedom
- Corruption and betrayal of trust for personal gain
- Scientific innovation as both liberation and weapon
- Sacrifice and heroism in the face of overwhelming power
- Parallel struggles between species - humans and Polyans both facing oppression
- The moral responsibility of creators toward their creations
- Resourcefulness and ingenuity as tools of resistance
- The corrupting influence of absolute power
- Truth and evidence versus deception and conspiracy
- Collective action and solidarity against authoritarian control
- The weaponization of peaceful technology
- Class struggle between workers and military/ruling castes

## Settings

### Earth

**Hôtel de Ville de Bruxelles:** Tower building in Brussels, Belgium where 25th Solvay Conference on Physics was held. Has balcony overlooking city where Max observed street conflicts.

**University of Connecticut Health Center:** Medical-dental school built 1970 on highest point in Hartford County. Expanded in 90s to include clinical research and private research projects. Tall buildings with lit windows at night. Contains Lyman Maynard Stowe Library, Academic Entrance, and Spheria Project facilities.

**Lyman Maynard Stowe Library:** Library at UConn Health Center with sculptures called 'Spheres' by Wopo Holop outside Academic Entrance. Site where Jill committed suicide by jumping, landing on sculpture pedestal. Has cobblestone circle entry.

**Spheria Project Office:** Research facility at UConn Health Center. Has makeshift lobby/reception area, conference room, kitchen, cubicles, individual offices. Team holds Friday happy hours with ethnic food and drinks. Engineers and observers work here studying virtual Spheria world.

**UConn Health Center Roof:** Rooftop with mooring mast disguised as cell tower, hosting Graham's airship 'Buruwa Gunya'

**Buruwa Gunya Airship:** Yellow luxury airship with open floor plan gondola, sunken booths, kitchen, private quarters, slanted windows

**Electric Boat Pier 3, Groton CT:** Submarine dock at Shaw Cove with Virginia-class submarine, yellow crane, cargo containers

**Secret server room:** Hidden duplicate server room below Spheria lab floor with red lighting, running Tor network and Gridway distributed computing for DARPA weapons project

**Hospital incinerator chamber:** Propane-fueled incinerator where Dana traps Min and Max; contains corpse on gurney, exhaust chimney with clean-out door

### Spheria

**Spheria:** Virtual world inside a computer sphere containing complete ecosystem. Has two hemispheres separated by the Rift. Governed by 'The Source' - a bright orb that compels behavior and changes color (white for travel, dimming for night). Landscape of brown rock with blue crystal mountains that emit repulsive force. Red rivers flow across surface. Inhabited by crystalline beings including Polyans.

**The Source:** Bright shining orb above Spheria that governs behavior of all creatures. Changes color - white during day/travel time, dims at night. Considered divine by Polyans. Large enough to contain games played by gods according to Polyan mythology.

**Polyan Colony:** Settlement of Polyans with hives (igloo-like pointed structures). Central gathering area. Organized by caste system (Soldiers, Drones, Builders, Leaders). Location where most Polyans live and feel secure.

**Blue Mountains:** Mountain ranges made of blue crystal that emit repulsive force preventing physical contact. Create maze-like divisions of world, making many sections unreachable. On one side has platforms/steps, on other side nearly vertical. Contains Uplift Pass - a large slit passage through mountains.

**The Rift:** Massive chasm dividing Spheria into two hemispheres. So deep and dark that its bottom cannot be seen. Objects dropped in (like yellow shards) transform and return as bubbles. Most prominent landmark, visible as vertical line from distance.

**Valley of Three:** Large perfect triangle formation of mountains on opposite hemisphere from Colony, visible in night sky. Contains no red rivers inside. Used by Soldiers for navigation. According to mythology, where gods play games - sometimes visible as flickering dots.

**Uplift Pass:** Large slit/crevice in blue mountains named by ancestors. Passage used by Lumen Seekers to access other side of mountains. Creates almost imperceptible push when entering. Part of traditional Lumen Walk training.

**Lumen Grotto:** Hexagonal dwelling far from Colony with vertical sides like cut hill. Contains glowing yellow crystal in center. Resting place where confident young Polyans who venture far eventually find it. Site of first test for Lumen Seeker selection.

**The Fertile Field:** Legendary location of endless energy. Not always present in same place. Recently discovered by Sa·ma after leaving Le·ma at camp. Source of Sa·ma's new sensor artifact.

**Golden Cave:** Cave with golden/metallic walls that reflect light, contains glowing plants, maze of passages, portal to Qubessence

**Council Building Prison Cell:** Triangular room tapering to point, former shelter against Zalisk, now first Polyan prison, contains hexagonal pit

**Tower bridge across the Rift:** Fallen tower now forming a bridge spanning the great Rift chasm, allowing Workers to cross to freedom

## Factions

### Polyan Colony (Spheria)
Organized society of crystalline Polyans divided into castes: three-legged Drones (laborers), four-legged Builders (construction), five-legged Soldiers (hunters/warriors), six-legged Leaders (governance), and special role of Lumen Master (five-legged with enhanced knowledge). Build hive structures, hunt for survival, maintain social order.
Key Members: Sa·ma, Le·ma, Drones (caste), Builders (caste), Soldiers (caste), Leaders (caste)

### Lumen Seekers (Spheria)
Special role within Polyan society. Individuals trained in arcane knowledge about world's nature and history. Undergo four tests including finding Lumen Grotto, Lumen Walk to Rift and back. Hold status equal to or higher than six-legged Leaders. Pass knowledge down through apprenticeship.
Key Members: Sa·ma, Le·ma

### Spheria Project Team (Earth)
Research group at UConn Health Center studying virtual world containing Polyan civilization. Mix of particle physicists, computer scientists, engineers, and observers. Theory: simulated worlds can provide proof of new fundamental particles. Hold Friday happy hours. Forbidden from interacting with Polyans during observation.
Key Members: Max, Min, Timothy, Jean, Jill (deceased, former intern)

### 25th Solvay Conference attendees (Earth)
Leading particle physicists gathered in Brussels. Historical conference dating to 1911 with luminaries like Einstein, Marie Curie, Poincaré. Max presented his virtual world theory to this group as rookie, impressing them despite skepticism.
Key Members: Max, David (chair)

### Fa·ro's Coalition (Spheria)
Growing faction supporting Fa·ro's leadership, believes Leaders are weak and indecisive
Key Members: Fa·ro, Ga·zo, Za·zo

### Liberated Workers (Spheria)
Workers who escaped Fa·ro's tyranny by crossing tower bridge over the Rift to unexplored territory
Key Members: Sa·ma, Hi·ma, Pu·ma, Le·ma, majority of Worker caste

### DARPA conspirators (Earth)
Secret military group funding weapons research through Spheria Project via Dana's betrayal
Key Members: Dana Carter, unknown DARPA officials

## Key Objects

### Violet crystals
Core life-giving crystals at heart of all Spherian creatures. Pulse with light. Without them, creatures would be mere inanimate structures of connected tetrahedrons. Large ones power massive beasts like Zalisks.
Significance: Fundamental source of life/animation for all beings in Spheria. Primary target of Polyan hunters.

### Green crystals
Buried crystals containing seed energy that creatures like Zalisks forage for by digging.
Significance: Food source for certain Spherian creatures, particularly Zalisks.

### Yellow crystals/shards
Glowing energy crystals. Small ones used in Polyan rituals. When dropped into Rift, transform and return as yellow bubbles. Found in Lumen Grotto and Fertile Field.
Significance: Used in Lumen Seeker training and rituals. Transform in Rift demonstrating mysterious properties of that location.

### Blue crystals
Crystal comprising mountains. Emit repulsive force preventing physical contact. Can be manipulated by those with proper knowledge. Create maze-like world divisions.
Significance: Fundamental geographic feature controlling movement and access in Spheria. Can be manipulated as part of advanced Polyan knowledge.

### Red rivers
Flowing red liquid traversing Spheria landscape. Can be crossed via bridges built by Polyans. Glow illuminates blue mountains at night.
Significance: Geographic features, sources of light, obstacles requiring engineering to cross.

### Indigo sensor with three triangles
New sensory organ acquired by Sa·ma from Fertile Field. Unlike normal sensors (eyes, ears, mouths), has three triangles pointing different directions. Grants enhanced abilities including commanding other creatures.
Significance: Artifact proving Fertile Field's existence. Grants Sa·ma god-like powers. Will be passed to Le·ma when she becomes Master.

### Spheres sculptures
Art installation by Wopo Holop at UConn Health Center library entrance. Two pedestals with bronze objects including human retina, dividing frog eggs, Earth, acorn, DNA strand, faceted and smooth spheres.
Significance: Location where Jill's body landed after suicide. Thematic connection to Spheria project's focus on spherical worlds and life.

### Spy glasses
Thick-rimmed glasses with hidden camera purchased from spygear.com. Min's favorite gadget, wears them regularly to record.
Significance: Allowed Min to record and show her interview, demonstrating her tech-savvy nature.

### VR headset and 3D SpaceMouse
Equipment used by Spheria Project observers to navigate virtual world. SpaceMouse looks like truck towing ball with programmable buttons around grip. Allows 3D movement and rotation.
Significance: Primary interface for humans to observe and navigate Spheria world without direct interaction.

### Electronic cigarette
Vapor device used by Max for nicotine to calm nerves.
Significance: Character detail showing Max's stress management and modern habits.

### Blue Rock Wall
Wall constructed along red river to prevent it reaching Colony dwellings
Significance: One of Sa·ma's insightful designs protecting the Colony

### Seven-sided Core Crystal
Empty crystal prepared for creating new seven-legged Polyan
Significance: Central component in breeding ritual to create another god-like being

### Glowing Plants in Golden Cave
Plants that emit light beams when broken, can heal themselves
Significance: Used by Sa·ma to navigate cave passages and find portal room

### Keurig Coffee Machine
Coffee maker with yellow blinking LEDs, uses Dark Embers k-cups
Significance: Min's companion during late night work sessions

### Sa·ma's beam cannon
Blue rock device with rope mechanism that fires projectiles; used to destroy tower supports and launch Le·ma through air
Significance: Key to Workers' rebellion - destroys tower to create bridge and delivers Le·ma to steal Fa·ro's artifact

### Fa·ro's artifact
Device allowing Fa·ro to scan for living creatures (violet) and structures (yellow); controls Zalisk herd
Significance: Symbol of Fa·ro's power; when stolen by Le·ma, turns Zalisk against Soldiers enabling Worker escape

### Blue rock barrier
Continuous wall of vertical blue rock created by Workers perpendicular to Soldier advance
Significance: Demonstrates Workers' advanced planning and construction abilities in rebellion against Fa·ro

### Red server cables
Bundle of red cables descending through hatch to secret server room, propping lid open
Significance: Physical evidence of DARPA weapons project and Dana's betrayal

## Chapter Synopses

### Ch. 1: Shard of Life [Spherian]
Nine five-limbed Soldier caste Polyans hunt a massive six-legged Zalisk beast. The creature forages for green crystals containing seed energy, governed by the Source orb above. Polyans work as team to hunt the violet crystal at the Zalisk's heart that gives it life. All beings in this world are made of connected tetrahedrons animated by core crystals.

### Ch. 2: 1% Inspiration, 99% Funding [Human]
Max, a particle physicist, steps onto balcony at Hôtel de Ville de Bruxelles after presenting at 25th Solvay Conference on Physics. His friend David, conference chair, congratulates him. Max reflects on historical luminaries who attended first 1911 conference. They discuss Max's theory about discovering proof of new fundamental particle through study of virtual worlds, which most physicists dismiss despite being intrigued.

### Ch. 3: Uplift Pass [Spherian]
Le·ma follows her mentor Sa·ma far from Colony to blue crystal mountains. He leads her to Uplift Pass, a large crevice allowing passage through otherwise impassable mountains. Sa·ma reveals this is part of her training to become next Lumen Master, a status equal to six-legged Leaders. They enter the passage which creates almost imperceptible push.

### Ch. 4: Cell Division [Human]
Timothy and Jill arrive at UConn Health Center's Lyman Maynard Stowe Library to study. Tim stops to tie his shoe near 'Spheres' sculptures. He hears loud smack, feels wetness on face, sees blood on his new white sneakers. Jill screams shrilly. She has apparently jumped to her death, landing on sculpture pedestal.

### Ch. 5: Arcane Physics [Spherian]
Sa·ma demonstrates descent down steep mountain slope using repulsive field of blue crystals to glide/bounce. Le·ma must follow same dangerous route as there's no passage back to Colony side. After initial terror and tumbling, Le·ma learns to control her descent by steering through repulsive fields and using flat outcroppings. They head toward the Rift before nightfall.

### Ch. 6: First Impressions [Human]
Max waits impatiently for slow elevator after kids press all buttons as prank. He arrives late to interview candidate for intern position (vacant six weeks after previous intern Jill's suicide). Max is frustrated by inability to unselect elevator floors, comparing to computer interface design. Eventually reaches floor seven for interview.

### Ch. 7: The Interview [Human]
Min returns to apartment, tells Australian roommate Sheila about amazing interview for dream internship. Project involves watching virtual world with computer creatures called Spherians (specifically intelligent ones called Polyans). Min uses her spy glasses to show Sheila recording of interview. They watch footage showing the Spheria virtual world and explanation of project.

### Ch. 8: The Rift [Spherian]
Sa·ma and Le·ma camp at the Rift as Source fades to night. Le·ma places yellow shard in circle Sa·ma traced. They observe opposite hemisphere with its blue mountain lines and red rivers. Le·ma asks about Valley of Three (perfect triangle visible in sky, mythological site where gods play games). Sa·ma explains no Polyan has reached opposite side. He describes Rift's purpose and mythology.

### Ch. 9: First Day [Human]
Min arrives for first day at Spheria Project. Max shows her to reception desk which will be her workstation (visitors rare, about one per month). He teaches her to log in and access data files. At 11am team meeting, Min meets project leader and other team members. She's assigned to analyze Polyan population data and create charts tracking different castes over time.

### Ch. 10: The Fertile Field [Spherian]
Flashback to young Sa·ma's exploration beyond Colony. He discovers hill-like structure with vertical sides containing hexagonal room. Inside, old feeble Polyan sits by glowing yellow crystal in the Lumen Grotto. Elder was expecting Sa·ma, explains confident Polyans eventually find this place as first test. Sa·ma claims he came seeking elder. Elder asks how he seeks without knowing what he seeks. Sa·ma answers: observation, studying patterns, applying knowledge. Elder confirms Sa·ma passed test and reveals there are four tests total.

### Ch. 11: Min's Assignment [Human]
Min arrives at work with coffee, receives message from Max to see him. In his office, she browses his books while he prepares files. She finds his favorite: 'A Short History of Nearly Everything' by Bill Bryson, which Max reads yearly. He wishes for updated edition covering quantum physics. Max gives Min assignment analyzing Polyan population data, creating charts of different castes over time using memory card of data.

### Ch. 12: Return Journey [Spherian]
Le·ma wakes to find Sa·ma gone, left alone as part of training. Yellow bubble rises from Rift depths. She follows their tracks back, finding travel easier and pleasant alone. Reaches red river where their bridge remains. Decides to slow down and observe environment more carefully. Notices unusual water plants she's never seen before near river.

Min finishes work at 6:12pm, stretches, hears blender signaling team happy hour. She brings Vietnamese Goi Cuon (salad rolls) with Nuoc cham sauce to share. Max tries and loves them. Tim makes frozen drinks in blender. Team gathers with various ethnic dishes for Friday social event. Casual, friendly atmosphere among Spheria Project colleagues.
