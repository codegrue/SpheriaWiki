# Spheria — Wiki
*by Cody Leet*

## Overall Plot

Spheria concludes by interweaving parallel revolutions on Earth and Spheria. On Earth, Min discovers Dana's secret DARPA weapons project hidden beneath the Qube floor, using Olivia's Wow Signal password to access Tor networks and military computing systems. When Max discovers Min in the secret room, they fight before realizing both are Dana's victims. Dana, exposed by her own greed for Costa Rica land and military funding, murders Olivia to protect the conspiracy and traps Min and Max in a hospital incinerator to eliminate witnesses before her board presentation. The duo escapes through chimney exhausts with hospital volunteer Walter's help. Meanwhile on Spheria, Fa·ro eliminates the Council to become sole dictator, enslaving Workers to build an impossible tower reaching the Source. But Sa·ma secretly engineers a revolutionary cannon that topples the tower across the Rift, creating a bridge to freedom. When Fa·ro's Soldiers attack, Le·ma launches herself through Sa·ma's cannon to steal Fa·ro's Zalisk-controlling artifact, turning his own weapons against him. The Zalisk herd tramples the Soldiers while Workers escape across the fallen tower bridge to liberation beyond the Rift. As Graham and the team watch recordings of both the Polyan revolution and Dana's confession video, the novel's central themes crystallize: the parallel struggles against tyranny, the corrupting nature of unchecked power, and how revolutionary ingenuity and sacrifice can overcome oppression. The Polyans' physical escape across the Rift mirrors humanity's moral reckoning with weaponizing their creation, as both civilizations confront whether freedom or control will define their futures.

## Themes

- Revolution and liberation from tyranny
- Corruption of scientific ideals by military-industrial complex
- Parallel struggles between artificial and human civilizations
- Sacrifice and heroism in face of oppression
- Betrayal of trust for personal gain
- Ingenuity and engineering as tools of freedom
- Price of unchecked authoritarian power
- Moral responsibility for created intelligence
- Technology as weapon versus tool of liberation
- Cost of bearing witness and speaking truth

## Settings

### Earth

**UConn Health Center:** Medical-dental school in Hartford County, Connecticut, built 1970. Expanded in 90s for clinical research. Houses the Spheria Project. Contains Lyman Maynard Stowe Library.

**Hôtel de Ville de Bruxelles:** Brussels City Hall tower where 25th Solvay Conference on Physics was held. Max presented here and met with David on the balcony overlooking the city.

**Spheria Project Office:** Research facility at UConn Health Center. Contains reception desk, conference room, cubicles, kitchen, and offices. Team holds Friday happy hours with ethnic food and frozen drinks.

**UConn Health Center rooftop:** Building rooftop with mooring mast disguised as cell tower antenna; Graham's yellow luxury airship 'Buruwa Gunya' docked here; accessible via emergency exit stairway

**Buruwa Gunya airship interior:** Enormous luxury airship gondola with open floor plan, slanted wraparound windows, sunken booths, kitchen with stainless steel appliances, and private rooms toward rear

**Electric Boat Pier 3, Groton CT:** Dark pier with Virginia-class submarine, large yellow crane, shipping containers; location of secret meeting in rain

**Secret server room:** Hidden duplicate of main server room beneath Qube floor, bathed in red light with cables and Tor network equipment for DARPA weapons project

**Hospital incinerator:** Crematorium chamber where Min and Max become trapped after Dana locks them inside

### Spheria

**The Colony:** Central Polyan settlement where the crystalline beings live in hives. Contains Drones, Builders, Soldiers, and is led by six-legged Leaders. Center gathering place for safety at night.

**Uplift Pass:** Large slit/passage through the blue crystal mountains. Named by ancient ancestors. Used by Lumen Masters to train apprentices. Leads to forbidden knowledge areas.

**The Rift:** Massive dark chasm at the edge of the world. Most prominent landmark in Spheria. Yellow crystals thrown in turn into bubbles. Marks boundary of known territory.

**Blue Mountains:** Mountain ranges made of blue crystal that emit repulsive force. Impassable barriers that divide the world into maze-like sections. Nearly vertical on some sides.

**Valley of Three:** Perfect triangle formation of mountains visible on opposite hemisphere at night. No red rivers inside. Used by Soldiers for navigation. Legendarily where gods play games.

**The Source:** Bright shining orb above the world that governs Polyan behavior. Changes color (white during day). Center of the spherical world where observers can float.

**Red Rivers:** Glowing red rivers that flow through the landscape. Can be crossed by building bridges. Illuminate the blue mountains at night from opposite hemisphere.

**Lumen Grotto:** Hexagonal dwelling far from the Colony with vertical sides like a cut hill. Resting place where old Lumenary waited for Sa·ma. Contains yellow glowing crystal.

**The Fertile Field:** Legendary field of endless energy that Sa·ma discovered. Not always present/visible. Location where Sa·ma received his mysterious third sensor gift.

**Cave system with glowing plants:** Underground cave with strange glowing plants that emit light beams when broken; twisting maze of passages; contains portal room; discovered by Sa·ma near mountains beyond river

**Portal room in cave:** Cave room containing portal to 'Qubessence'; where Sa·ma encountered cave god; location Fa·ro demands to visit

**Council building prison cell:** Triangular room tapering to point; hollow interior of Council building; former shelter against Zalisk attacks; now Spheria's first prison; contains shallow hexagonal pit in center; single large door, no windows

**Fallen tower bridge:** The great tower toppled across the Rift by Sa·ma's cannon, creating a bridge for Workers to escape

## Factions

### Polyan Castes (Spheria)
Hierarchical society of crystalline beings divided by number of legs: three-legged Drones (workers), four-legged Builders (construction), five-legged Soldiers (hunters), six-legged Leaders (governance).
Key Members: Le·ma, Sa·ma, Drones, Builders, Soldiers, Leaders

### Lumen Seekers (Spheria)
Elite group of Polyans who possess secret knowledge about the world. Led by Lumen Master, pass down ancient wisdom through apprenticeship. Special status equal to or higher than six-legged Leaders.
Key Members: Sa·ma (Lumen Master), Le·ma (apprentice), unnamed old Polyan at Lumen Grotto

### Spheria Project Team (Earth)
Group of scientists and researchers at UConn Health Center studying the artificial Spheria ecosystem. Includes physicists, computer scientists, and observers. Socially awkward but passionate.
Key Members: Max (lead), Min (intern), Jean (observer), Tim, David (conference chair)

### Fa·ro's Faction (Spheria)
Pro-Fa·ro group believing he brings decisive leadership to previously gridlocked Council; see him as god who makes Colony unstoppable; includes Ga·zo and Za·zo as loyalists
Key Members: Fa·ro, Ga·zo, Za·zo

### Council Leaders (Spheria)
Eight six-legged Polyans who govern Colony; often divided and gridlocked; increasingly defer to Fa·ro to break ties; named Wu·sa, Yo·sa, Me·sa, Ju·sa, Ki·sa, Co·sa, Pi·sa, Vu·sa
Key Members: Wu·sa, Yo·sa, Me·sa, Ju·sa, Ki·sa, Co·sa, Pi·sa, Vu·sa

### Escaped Workers (Spheria)
Workers who crossed the fallen tower bridge to freedom beyond the Rift under Sa·ma's leadership
Key Members: Sa·ma, Hi·ma, Pu·ma, Le·ma

### DARPA conspirators (Earth)
Military and corporate alliance secretly developing Spheria as weapons research despite Graham's contract prohibitions
Key Members: Dana

## Key Objects

### Violet Core Crystal
Life-giving crystal in the center of all Spherian creatures. Without it, they become inanimate tetrahedron structures. The Polyans hunt Zalisks for these.
Significance: Fundamental to life in Spheria—the source of animation and consciousness for all beings

### Green Crystals
Buried crystals containing seed energy that creatures like Zalisks forage for food.
Significance: Primary food source for herbivorous Spherian creatures

### Yellow Crystal Shard
Small yellow crystals carried by Lumen Seekers. When thrown into the Rift, transform into bubbles that slowly rise.
Significance: Used in Lumen Master rituals and tests; demonstrates mysterious properties of the Rift

### Blue Crystal Mountains
Mountains made of repulsive blue crystal that push beings away, creating impassable barriers.
Significance: Divide the world into regions; fundamental geographic feature that shapes Polyan civilization

### Third Sensor
Mysterious indigo sensor with three triangles that Sa·ma received as a 'gift from the gods.' Grants telepathic/command abilities.
Significance: Artifact that gives Lumen Masters enhanced powers to influence creatures and perceive the world

### Spy Glasses
Thick-rimmed glasses worn by Min that secretly record video. She uses them to document everything.
Significance: Allow Min to capture footage of the Spheria simulation and her interviews

### VR Headset and SpaceMouse
Virtual reality equipment and 3D controller used by researchers to observe and navigate the Spheria simulation.
Significance: Interface technology that allows humans to view and move through the artificial world

### Spheres Sculptures
Bronze sculptures by Wopo Holop at UConn showing retina, dividing frog eggs, Earth, acorn, DNA strand, and faceted spheres on pedestals.
Significance: Ironic location where Jill committed suicide; symbolic of life, cells, and worlds

### Buruwa Gunya airship
Graham's enormous yellow luxury airship with red script name; has mooring capability; gondola interior with open floor plan, wraparound windows, sunken booths, kitchen
Significance: Graham's personal transportation and mobile office; demonstrates his wealth and unconventional style

### Virginia-class submarine photonics mast
Low-profile mast with camera array connected by fiber optic cables; resembles traditional periscope; no hull penetration strengthens structural integrity; may have 360-degree camera
Significance: Advanced submarine technology observed at secret pier meeting location

### Seven-sided core crystal
Empty crystal with seven facets used in Polyan creation ceremony; placed in center of Council Chamber; seven orange elongated crystals positioned near each facet
Significance: Ritual object for creating seven-legged Polyan gods; used to make second god after Fa·ro

### Glowing cave plants
Strange plants in underground cave that emit light beams when broken; can heal themselves; used by Sa·ma to illuminate cave passages
Significance: Mysterious natural lighting system in cave; indicates unusual environment near portal to Qubessence

### Yellow shard light source
Small glowing yellow crystal used by Ga·zo to illuminate Sa·ma's prison cell
Significance: Portable Polyan light source used in dark enclosed spaces

### Sa·ma's cannon
Blue rock device that fires beams or launches objects, used to topple tower and create defensive wall
Significance: Revolutionary weapon enabling Workers to escape Fa·ro's tyranny by bridging the Rift

### Fa·ro's artifact
Device allowing visual scanning and control of Zalisk herds, outlined in yellow and violet
Significance: Symbol of power that Le·ma steals to turn Fa·ro's own weapon against him

### Tor network equipment
Untraceable dark web communication system in secret server room
Significance: Evidence of Dana's covert DARPA weapons research using Spheria

### Confession video
Recording of Dana admitting to Olivia's murder and trapping Max
Significance: Max's insurance exposing Dana's conspiracy before board meeting

## Chapter Synopses

### Ch. 1: Shard of Life [Spherian]
Nine Polyan Soldiers hunt a massive Zalisk beast. The creature forages for green crystals containing seed energy. The Polyans target the violet crystal in the Zalisk's core that gives it life. They work as a coordinated team, having learned from past deaths.

### Ch. 2: 1% Inspiration, 99% Funding [Human]
Max stands on a balcony in Brussels after presenting at the 25th Solvay Conference on Physics. His friend David, the conference chair, congratulates him. Max reflects on being a rookie impressing leading particle physicists and his admiration for historical figures like Einstein and Curie.

### Ch. 3: Uplift Pass [Spherian]
Le·ma follows Sa·ma far from the Colony to a mountain range. They reach Uplift Pass, a large slit in the blue crystal mountains. Sa·ma reveals this passage leads to secret knowledge passed down through generations of Lumen Masters.

### Ch. 4: Cell Division [Human]
Timothy and Jill walk to the UConn library at night to study. As Tim bends to tie his shoe near the 'Spheres' sculptures, Jill is killed in a suicide jump from above. Tim is splattered with blood and hears her scream.

### Ch. 5: Arcane Physics [Spherian]
Le·ma must descend the steep mountain slope after Sa·ma jumps. She learns to navigate using the repulsive force of blue crystals, bouncing and gliding down flat outcroppings. Sa·ma reveals there's no passage back from this side—finding the return route is one of her tests.

### Ch. 6: First Impressions [Human]
Max waits impatiently for a slow elevator where kids pressed all the buttons. He's late for interviewing a candidate to replace their previous intern who committed suicide. He frustratedly wishes elevators had undo buttons like computer interfaces.

### Ch. 7: The Interview [Human]
Min returns home and tells her roommate Sheila about the amazing internship interview. She explains the Spheria virtual world with computer creatures called Spherians/Polyans. Min shows Sheila footage from her spy glasses of the interview and the simulation.

### Ch. 8: The Rift [Spherian]
Le·ma and Sa·ma camp beside the massive Rift chasm at world's edge. They observe the opposite hemisphere at night, including the Valley of Three triangle formation. Sa·ma explains the Rift's significance and they prepare to test if a yellow crystal will transform when thrown in.

### Ch. 9: First Day [Human]
Min starts her internship working at the reception desk, which rarely has visitors. Max introduces her to the team at the 11am meeting. She meets the socially awkward scientists who make up the Spheria Project staff.

### Ch. 10: The Fertile Field [Spherian]
Flashback to young Sa·ma's exploration beyond the Colony. He discovers the Lumen Grotto, a hexagonal dwelling with a glowing yellow crystal. An old Polyan was expecting him, explaining this is the first test of becoming a Lumen Seeker.

### Ch. 11: Min's Assignment [Human]
Min meets with Max who gives her an assignment to analyze data files. He shows her his book collection including his favorite 'A Short History of Nearly Everything' by Bill Bryson, which he reads yearly and wishes had a quantum physics chapter.

### Ch. 12: Return Journey [Spherian]
Le·ma wakes to find Sa·ma has left her alone. She observes a yellow bubble rising from the Rift, possibly the transformed crystal. She begins her solo return journey, taking time to observe her surroundings and reflect on her learning. She encounters a peaceful pond.

### Ch. 13: Happy Hour [Human]
At 6:12pm Friday happy hour, Min brings Vietnamese Goi Cuon (salad rolls) with Nuoc cham sauce. Max tries and loves them. Tim makes frozen drinks with a blender. The team socializes over ethnic food and beverages.

### Ch. 14: Extra Sense [Spherian]
A Zalisk about to kill Le·ma suddenly obeys Sa·ma's telepathic command and walks away. Sa·ma reveals a new third sensor with three triangles—an artifact gift from the gods. He discovered the legendary Fertile Field and gained this ability to communicate and influence creatures.

### Ch. 15: Starlight Walk [Human]
Max and Min leave the office drunk and walk through the parking lot at night. Max drunkenly sits in an unlocked Mercedes. They eventually walk down to look at the pond, Max staggering between parked cars in the medical staff lot.

### Ch. 16: Cliffhanger [Spherian]
Le·ma and Sa·ma discuss how to return over the nearly vertical mountains. Sa·ma reveals there's no passage on this side—Le·ma must create a way over. He hints she should use what she knows, letting slip they need to 'make it to the other side.'

### Ch. 17: God from the Machine [Human]
Jean uses VR headset and SpaceMouse to observe the Polyan Colony in the simulation. She flies through the virtual world, watching Drones and Builders construct a new igloo-like hive. She wants to help but is forbidden from interacting—only engineers can.

### Ch. 18: Prime Directive [Human]
Dana reviews weekly logs showing increased unusual Polyan activity. For first time, anthropology log entries exceed engineering entries. Jean notes Polyans seem to be moving toward 'new social zeitgeist' with most interesting behaviors happening collectively rather than individually.

### Ch. 19: Crossing Over [Human]
Max drives to UConn Health Center and is amazed to see Graham's enormous yellow luxury airship 'Buruwa Gunya' moored atop building. Due to Ponzo Illusion, airship appears even larger than it is. Max reflects on visual perception tricks.

### Ch. 20: Running Out [Human]
Dana and Max climb to roof and board Graham's luxury airship via gangway. Interior is massive gondola with open floor plan, wraparound windows, sunken booths, kitchen, and private rooms. They are impressed by the scale.

### Ch. 21: Cat and Mouse [Human]
In rain at night, mysterious driver in black sedan arrives at Electric Boat pier in Groton CT. Virginia-class submarine docked nearby. Driver in hooded rain jacket skulks toward shipping containers beneath large yellow crane.

### Ch. 22: In Our Image [Spherian]
Fourth consecutive red bubble rises toward Source. Eight Leaders (Wu·sa, Yo·sa, Me·sa, Ju·sa, Ki·sa, Co·sa, Pi·sa, Vu·sa) meet with Sa·ma in Council Chamber. Red river threatening Colony; they debate moving it. Me·sa claims gods are angry. Council divided on reasons but agrees Colony must relocate.

### Ch. 23: Nourishment [Human]
Max and Min have dinner date discussing religion and beliefs. Min was raised Catholic but doesn't dwell on it. Max believes universe is mathematical algorithm; if God exists, he just created fundamental rules and watches. They discuss self-assembling patterns in nature.

### Ch. 24: Pro Creation [Human]
Min visits Max's minimalist apartment with impressive entertainment center and video game systems. They discuss games; Min prefers building games like Minecraft. Max's dad doesn't like Minecraft because it has no story. They prepare to play together with beers.

### Ch. 25: Start the Press [Human]
Gizmodo article by Moses Diego titled 'Scientists Play God In This Virtual Experiment' describes visiting Max's lab. Article details Polyans as sentient polygonal creatures with caste system based on leg count (3-6), recent creation of seven-legged god figure, and introduces virtual world called Spheria.

### Ch. 26: Conspiracy [Spherian]
Fa·ro walks through empty Colony center and climbs central dais, feeling he should be revered more as god. He poses dramatically. He resents Council treating him like servant. He considers having Sa·ma build statue of him and schemes about maintaining his divine status.

### Ch. 27: The World Builder [Human]
Min examines data when Frankie startles her. They debate whether world is mathematical or magical. Min argues repeating patterns in nature follow equations. Frankie invites her to see real beauty in random patterns. He's going out to get textures for the project.

### Ch. 28: Streets of Gold [Spherian]
Sa·ma returns to Colony in daze and gathers audience of Drones including Ti·ni and Po·ni. He tells story of following river into wilderness, sensing something below ground, discovering large cave entrance on hillside. Cave was unlike any he'd seen before with unusual properties.

### Ch. 29: The Deleted File [Human]
Olivia Holland's work journal entries from 5/30 to later dates. First real job as intern doing statistical analysis. Boyfriend Randy breaks up with her via email after 6 years. She gets hungover at work. Becomes closer with roommate Ashley during recovery.

### Ch. 30: God Like You [Spherian]
Sa·ma leads Fa·ro, Ga·zo, and Za·zo into cave system. Fa·ro demands to reach portal to 'Qubessence' to join other gods, claiming it's his destiny. Sa·ma worried because cave god said 'come back when it's done' and didn't invite others. Floor rougher than Sa·ma remembers.

### Ch. 31: The Exchange [Human]
Ashley watches The Walking Dead alone during thunderstorm. She counts lightning distance. Rick Grimes sneaks through zombie-filled warehouse on screen. Knocking sound occurs but Rick doesn't react on TV - suggesting something unusual happening in real world.

### Ch. 32: Confinement [Spherian]
Sa·ma imprisoned in triangular cell - hollow interior of Council building, Spheria's first prison. Ga·zo visits with yellow shard light. Ga·zo loyal to Fa·ro, says Colony stronger than ever with god's leadership. Sa·ma warns nothing is unstoppable and criticizes closed minds.

### Ch. 33: Coffee Break [Human]
Min works late alone preparing for Graham's visit tomorrow. Everyone else gone home after working all night. Her eyes hurt from staring at numbers. She gets coffee from Keurig. Returns to find blue screen system failure requiring reboot. Computer won't respond to any commands.

### Ch. 34: Putsch [Spherian]
Leaders and Fa·ro prepare to create second seven-legged god. Ga·zo convinced them backup god would be wise. Council divided but Fa·ro broke tie by agreeing. They respect his apparent selflessness. Setup complete with seven-sided core crystal and seven orange leg crystals. Source shifts violet triggering replication urge.

### Ch. 35: The Shadow Room [Human]
Min discovers a hidden hatch beneath the Qube floor leading to a secret red-lit server room with mysterious cables and equipment she doesn't recognize.

### Ch. 36: Enslaved [Spherian]
Fa·ro declares himself sole ruler after eliminating the Council. He announces the Leaders are dead and forces the entire Colony to witness his ascension, with Sa·ma as captive witness.

### Ch. 37: Split Decision [Human]
Min accesses the secret terminal using Olivia's Wow Signal password. She discovers Gridway meta-scheduling software and Tor network connections, realizing this is military weapons research.

### Ch. 38: The Plan [Spherian]
Hi·ma and Pu·ma follow secret instructions to meet Sa·ma in darkness. Flashback reveals Sa·ma showing Fa·ro the tower design while secretly planning with Workers to topple it across the Rift.

### Ch. 39: Mala Sanctis [Human]
Min fights Max in the secret room after he discovers her. She kicks him, causing chaos with fallen Qubes and ladder. She escapes toward the elevator.

### Ch. 40: The Machine [Spherian]
Fa·ro discovers all Workers have vanished overnight. Using his artifact, he sees them gathered beyond the fallen tower with Sa·ma's cannon device.

### Ch. 41: Button Mashup [Human]
Min bursts into Dana's office warning about Max's weapon project and Olivia's murder. Dana locks the door and listens as Max pounds on it demanding entry.

### Ch. 42: Storm Wind [Spherian]
Ga·zo leads Soldiers pursuing the Workers but they encounter a blue rock wall erected perpendicular to their path - another of Sa·ma's defenses using the cannon.

### Ch. 43: The Incinerator [Human]
Confrontation between Max, Min, and Dana escalates. Max reveals he knew nothing about the secret room. Dana pulls a gun and forces them both into the hospital incinerator.

### Ch. 44: The Great Divide [Spherian]
Hi·ma enters the fallen tower now forming a bridge across the Rift. Sa·ma's cannon had destroyed support columns, causing the tower to topple perfectly across the chasm.

### Ch. 45: Ashes to Ashes [Human]
Trapped in the incinerator with a corpse, Min and Max work together. They discover an exhaust vent and Min climbs through to find a chimney clean-out door.

### Ch. 46: Into Battle [Spherian]
Le·ma uses Sa·ma's cannon to launch herself at Fa·ro during battle. Sa·ma fires her through the air and she collides with Fa·ro, stealing his artifact that controls Zalisk.

### Ch. 47: Clean-out [Human]
Hospital volunteer Walter hears banging from the incinerator chimney while transporting laundry. He investigates the morse-code-like pattern and unlocks the clean-out door.

### Ch. 48: Liberation [Spherian]
Using Fa·ro's stolen artifact, Le·ma commands Zalisk herd to attack only Soldiers. The Workers escape across the tower bridge while Soldiers are trampled and destroyed.

### Ch. 49: Projection [Human]
Dana presents quarterly report to Graham and team without Max. Graham serves her coffee and questions Max's absence, expressing concern about needing visionary leadership.

### Ch. 50: Interruptions [Human]
During presentation, screens show Dana's confession video admitting she murdered Olivia and trapped Max for DARPA funding and Costa Rica land. Captain Brennan arrives to arrest her but she pulls a gun.

### Ch. 51: Hand of God [Mixed]
Max and team watch Spherian recordings showing tower collapse and Zalisk battle. Graham witnesses Polyans' resourcefulness. The parallel between Polyan and human struggles for freedom becomes clear as both stories converge.
